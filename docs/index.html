GitHub Page

As the pandemic has shown, many workers essential to the functioning of cities are also some of our lowest paid. This interactive visualization design project intends to demonstrate how difficult it is to live as an essential worker in major U.S. cities to build a case for increasing the minimum wage.

Average Hourly Income Per Capita

According to U.S. Bereau of Labor Statistics , the U.S. national median annual income is $39,810 in 2019, while the median annual income of the essential workers is about $31,090. There are about 15 million essential workers in 2019, which makes up about 10.2% of the working population in the US. This number is continuously increasing.

Median Annual Wage in Major Cities

To further investigate, using the selection of categories as essential workers, the median annual salary could be cross-compared across 5 major cities. There are considerable variences in terms of the magnitude of the wage and its position among all essential workers' income in different locations. You could navigate through tabs on the right corner to check more information on the employment percentage of each major category of essential workers across cities (click employment by cities). Or to see more details where the major category is broken down to specific occupations, please check the More detail by cities. You might be able to draw your own conclusion on where to move and what job you should choose in terms of wage and employment trend among cities in discussion.

Cost of Living Index in Major U.S. Cities

Cost of living is a metric of the prices needed to cover basic expenses, and is often used to compared how expensive it is to live in one city compared to the other. A person living in a city with a higher cost of living will require more money to afford the exact same lifestyle as someone in a city with a lower cost of living.

A cost of living index is a number representing the relative price levels for consumer goods and services. While many such indexes exist, the Cost of Living Index compiled by the American Chamber of Commerce Researchers Association (ACCRA) and the Council for Community and Economic Research (C2ER) is one of the most widely accepted in the United States. It uses the average cost of living for all participaing areas, both metropolitan and nonmetropolitan, for the index of 100. Thus the index is read as a percentage of the average for all places.

The composite Cost of Living Index is the weighted average of the index calculated in different categories, such as housing and groceries.

Adjusted Poverty Line in Major U.S. Cities

Despite the Cost of Living index being a useful metric to compare different cities, it would be useful to have a monetary guideline to compare the annual wages of each occupation to. This is where the U.S. Federal Poverty Guidelines comes in. A family is considered to be living in poverty if their household income falls below the poverty line for their household size. Many government assistance programs will help families whose total income falls below 200% of the poverty line.

A downside of the poverty line is that it does not adjust for cost of living. It's the same everywhere in the continental U.S. It would be interesting to use the Cost of Living Index to adjust the poverty level for each city of interest, since the COL Index is a percentage of the average cost of living in the U.S.

Insight

  • As the U.S. is facing the Covid-19 pandemic, the life of essential workers are getting even tougher. Retail stores are closing and companies are reducing their employment numbers. Schools are closing and many essential workers have to work while taking care of their children. Unlike non-essential workers who can work from home, many essential workers has job that requires them to interact with people, which undoubtedly put their personal health at risk. Research has shown that for the people who got infected with Covid-19, large porportion of them are essential workers or had contact with them.
  • Our society not only needs more awareness about the living situations of essential workers, but also need to improve social welfare. For example, the government could
  • - provide a more affordable healthcare for essential workers
  • - enforce a stricter safety and health standard to reduce the risk of getting infected
  • - provide child care oppotunities for families of essential workers
  • - help improve the current laws and regulations to help protect the rights of the essential workers
  • - shed light on decision making progress in customization of support system for low income community based on aera where they locate and updates accordingly with evolving living conditions during severe nature or ecnomic crisis
  • etc.

About us

Our Mission

It is never easy to make a living in a city, especially for those essential workers who are trying to settle down in those major cities in the United States. Moreover, as the pandemic precede, some of the lowest paid in the cities have also experienced a heavy financial impact under the lockdown policy and economic regression. Therefore, by taking a further exploration into the wages and living expenditures of some essential occupations with relatively lower income, we are trying to find out where their living pressure is mostly coming from, and finally come up with individual or governmental advice to help dealing with their problems and improving their life quality.

Our Design

In this project, we picked five major cities for further exploration: Seattle(Northwest), Los Angeles(Southwest), Chicago(Central North), Dallas(Central South), and New York(North East). The geographic span of the five major cities almost covers the whole territory of the US. Also, each city has unique features in its culture, politics, economic condition and so on, so that it is worth comparing. Therefore, we are having these five cities as representatives in our analysis. Besides the selections of cities, the major categories of essential workers were following the guideline provided by National Conference of State Legislatures. The major category were further broken down to specific occupations defined in Standard Occupational Classification (SOC) system. One thing to point out is that the essential worker defination differs in different state. The occupations included here is only an overall estimate for ublic education purpose and could not reflect exact situation in each state.

Our Website

  • We first begin with a story from the perspective of an American citizen who would like to choose an occupation to earn money and a city to live in. The introduction in the form of story-telling can give the viewers a realistic feeling and lead them to go through the upcoming exploration process. Then we make a nationwide analysis on the average hourly income to show the wage distribution.
  • Afterwards, we highlight the each selected city on the clickable map to show its median annual wage by giving visualizations of its trend through years and breakdown in different occupation categories. To make a more contrast and vivid visualization, we also use a bar chart to compare the annual income for each occupation. The viewers can play with the filter to change the year or occupation through the bar and the drop-down list.
  • Next, we break down the cost of living into a pie chart to show the proportion of each category in the total life expenditure. We also use bar chart to compare the cost of living index between cities. We would like to link the median annual wages we get from the last graphs to plug into the cost-of-living chart to see if the basic life expenditure can be covered or not.
  • We have used bootstrap framwork to ensure the readability and responsiveness on mobile device (different size of screen).
